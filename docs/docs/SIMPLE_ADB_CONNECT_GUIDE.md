# Simple Guide: Connect Galaxy XR to Cursor IDE

**Follow these steps exactly. Don't worry about anything else - just follow these steps.**

## Step 1: Open Extensions in Cursor

1. Look at the **left side** of Cursor (the sidebar)
2. Find the icon that looks like **4 squares** (or says "Extensions")
3. **Click on it**

   *If you can't find it:*
   - Press `Ctrl+Shift+X` on your keyboard
   - Or go to: **View** → **Extensions** (at the top menu)

## Step 2: Find Your ADB Extension

1. At the top of the Extensions panel, there's a **search box**
2. Type: `ADB` or `Android ADB` or `Wireless ADB`
3. Look for an extension that says something like:
   - "ADB WLAN"
   - "Android ADB"
   - "Wireless ADB"
   - "ADB Interface"

4. **Check if it says "Installed"** - if it does, you're good!
5. **If it's NOT installed**, click the "Install" button

## Step 3: Use the Extension to Connect

**Method A: Using Command Palette (Easiest)**

1. Press `Ctrl+Shift+P` on your keyboard
   - This opens a box at the top of the screen
   
2. Type: `ADB` or `Connect` or `Wireless`
   
3. Look for a command that says something like:
   - "ADB: Connect Device"
   - "ADB: Scan QR Code"
   - "ADB: Pair Device"
   - "Wireless ADB: Connect"
   
4. **Click on that command** (or press Enter)

5. A window or panel will open - follow what it says

**Method B: Using Extension Panel**

1. After opening Extensions (Step 1), click on your ADB extension
2. Look for buttons that say:
   - "Connect"
   - "Scan QR Code"
   - "Pair Device"
3. **Click that button**

**Method C: Look for Sidebar Icon**

1. Look at the **left sidebar** (where Extensions icon is)
2. See if there's a new icon that appeared (might look like a phone or Android logo)
3. **Click on it** - it should open an ADB panel
4. Look for a "Connect" or "Scan" button and click it

## Step 4: What Happens Next

- If it asks for a QR code: Point your camera/webcam at the QR code on your Galaxy XR
- If it asks for IP and code: Enter the information from your device
- Wait for it to say "Connected" or "Success"

## If You Can't Find the Extension

**Check if it's installed:**
1. Open Extensions (`Ctrl+Shift+X`)
2. Click "Installed" in the left sidebar
3. Search for "ADB" - do you see anything?

**If NO extension is installed:**
1. In Extensions, search for: `ADB WLAN` or `Android ADB`
2. Install one of these popular ones:
   - "ADB WLAN" by various authors
   - "Android ADB" 
   - "Wireless ADB"
3. After installing, **reload Cursor** (close and reopen, or press `Ctrl+Shift+P` → type "Reload Window")
